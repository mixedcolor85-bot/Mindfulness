Resilience

Stress prevention and reduction, as well as emergency coping strategies, can help minimize personal daily deterioration. 🔹 Cognitive Strategies 1️⃣ Cognitive Approach 1. Emotion Labeling * “Right now I feel anger and anxiety.” * Effect: Activates prefrontal cortex → inhibits amygdala 2. Self-distancing * “This stimulus and my response are separate.” * Effect: Suppresses automatic aggressive or overreactive behaviors 3. Cognitive Reappraisal * “I cannot change the environment, but I can control my response.” * Effect: Reduces helplessness and frustration 4. Grounding / Attention Shift * Focus on visual, auditory, tactile cues → be present * Effect: Suppresses overthinking & repetitive stress 2️⃣ Behavioral Strategies 1. Immediate routine actions * Drink water, close eyes, stretch * Effect: Restores sense of control, stabilizes nervous system 2. Small physical movements * Change posture, shake hands/feet, light exercise * Effect: Relieves muscle tension, calms sympathetic nervous system 3. Sensory stimulation * Cold water face/hand/foot, massage → quick calm 4. Maintain daily routine * Consistent sleep, meals, exercise * Effect: Keeps stability amid repetitive stress triggers 5. Emotional expression * Writing, speaking, shouting → releases suppressed anger/anxiety 💡 Summary: “Environment cannot be controlled, but cognitive distancing + emotion labeling + behavioral routines can prevent stress explosions and minimize daily deterioration.”
